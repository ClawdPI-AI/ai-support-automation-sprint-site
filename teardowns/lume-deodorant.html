Likely paths

What the audit would map first

  • Subscription management.
  • Order tracking and returns.
  • Product issue intake.
  • Scent dissatisfaction and 60-day return-policy questions.
  • Product safety, ingredients, pregnancy/breastfeeding, kids/teenagers, gluten, caffeine, and skin-sensitivity questions.
  • Retail-store vs website product-size differences.
  • Data privacy or deletion requests.
Safe opportunities

Where automation could help

  • Build category-specific intake for subscription, order, product issue, shipping, return feedback, and account problems.
  • Create a product-question assistant that answers only from approved FAQ snippets and routes health-sensitive questions to human review.
  • Add a scent/return flow that distinguishes website orders from retail purchases before promising return handling.
  • Create a product issue workflow that captures product name, batch/lot if available, photos, purchase channel, and symptoms, then escalates.
  • Add a "restricted claims" QA set for pregnancy, breastfeeding, kids, skin reactions, clinical claims, sunscreen/acidified products, and medical language.
Human-owned

Risk areas to keep human

  • Any medical, pregnancy, breastfeeding, child-use, adverse reaction, allergy, or injury-related question.
  • Clinical claim interpretation outside approved wording.
  • Refund exceptions and retail-vs-DTC purchase disputes.
  • Data privacy/deletion requests.
  • Product complaints that could become safety or regulatory issues.
