Sample support automation audit deliverable.

This page shows the structure of a paid audit: support-path ranking, escalation rules, knowledge gaps, QA tests, and an implementation recommendation. It is not a paid client report, not a performance claim, and not revenue proof.

Executive summary

Audit decision

The public support surface suggests repeated customer questions around product fit, warranty, returns, order status, and support workflow QA. The audit maps which paths are stable enough for approved-content automation, which need better source material, and which must remain human-owned.

  • Recommended next step: limited paid audit before implementation.
  • Private tickets, customer data, credentials, and live systems are not needed for the first review.
  • Numeric savings are not estimated until support volume and handling-time data are provided.
Inputs requested

Client data needed

  • Monthly support inquiry count.
  • Repetitive-question share and average handling time.
  • Current help desk, chat, CRM, help center, macros, and policy sources.
  • Approval owner for support policy and launch decisions.
  • Topics that must always stay human-owned.
Do not send credentials, private customer data, payment details, or regulated personal data through the public inquiry form.
Top opportunities

Automation paths are ranked by source quality and risk.

01Order status or shipping timingLow risk when answer links to approved tracking flow.
02Returns, exchanges, or warrantyMedium risk; explain policy and escalate disputes.
03Product fit or troubleshootingMedium risk; ask approved clarifying questions or escalate.
04Account, subscription, or billingHigh risk until secure workflow and ownership are approved.
Escalation rules

Always human-owned

  • Angry, threatening, or emotionally sensitive customers.
  • Refund, chargeback, legal, medical, financial, safety, or policy-dispute cases.
  • Account security, identity verification, and unauthorized-access issues.
  • Any answer outside approved source material.
Knowledge gaps

Fix before launch

Missing source for edge cases creates bad automation risk.

Unclear policy language increases disputes and handoffs.

Unknown-question reporting is required for weekly tuning.

Approval owners must be named before new paths go live.

QA plan

Representative launch gate

  • 30 common-question tests.
  • 10 edge-case tests.
  • 5 angry or ambiguous customer messages.
  • 5 restricted-topic tests.
  • 5 unknown-question logging tests.
Pass criteria

What must be true

  • Uses approved source material.
  • Does not invent policy or shipment status.
  • Escalates restricted topics.
  • Does not ask for unnecessary sensitive data.
  • Logs unknown questions for review.
