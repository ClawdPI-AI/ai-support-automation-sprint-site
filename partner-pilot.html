What the partner gets

A sellable audit packet, not a vague strategy note.

The output is designed so a partner can review it internally, share the safe portions with a client, and decide whether implementation should proceed.

Partner brief

Client-fit argument

Clear support automation angle, likely buyer owner, risk level, and recommended positioning for the partner's client conversation.

Audit map

Support-path ranking

Repeated support paths, source-of-truth gaps, safe automation candidates, and paths that should stay human-owned.

Implementation scope

Build or wait decision

QA tests, escalation rules, implementation backlog, and a recommendation to proceed, run a limited pilot, or clean up content first.

Five-day workflow

Structured enough to run repeatedly.

Day 1

Confirm client profile, support channels, help-center sources, risk boundaries, and buyer-facing use case.

Day 2

Map public support signals, repeated questions, likely source gaps, and escalation-sensitive topics.

Day 3

Draft automation opportunities, restricted-topic rules, and QA tests for common and edge-case requests.

Day 4

Package the partner-facing brief and client-ready audit deliverable with implementation options.

Day 5

Review changes, clarify risks, and finalize the implementation recommendation.

Scope rules

Commercially useful without overclaiming.

The pilot is intentionally bounded: it helps a partner sell a scoped next step, but it does not invent client proof or handle payment outside a legitimate contract route.

Not included
